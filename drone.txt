# Low-Cost Ocean Cleanup Drone

A project by a team of engineering students from Lendi Institute of Engineering and Technology, focused on creating affordable, practical solutions for environmental challenges.

---

## Project Overview

Plastic waste in water bodies is a growing environmental threat. Manual cleanup is expensive and slow. This project presents a low-cost, net-equipped drone designed to autonomously collect floating waste from lakes, rivers, or coastal areas.

## Objectives

- Develop an affordable drone for surface waste collection.
- Automate the process using a servo-actuated net.
- Provide real-time monitoring via FPV (First Person View) camera.
- Create a user-friendly and energy-efficient solution for water cleanup.

## How It Works

1. **Takeoff & Navigation:**  
   Manual or semi-autonomous control using the FlySky FS-i6 transmitter and Mission Planner software.

2. **Waste Detection & Collection:**  
   Pilot identifies debris via FPV camera. A servo lowers the collection net to scoop waste, then lifts it for transport.

3. **Waste Disposal:**  
   Drone flies to a disposal area, where the servo releases the collected waste.

4. **Return & Landing:**  
   Drone returns to the launch point for battery change or next mission.

---

## Assembly & Setup

- **Frame and Motors:** Mount motors on F450 frame.
- **ESCs:** Connect to motors and power distribution.
- **Flight Controller:** Wire up APM 2.8, calibrate with Mission Planner.
- **Net System:** Attach servo and net for debris collection.
- **FPV:** Install camera and receiver for live video.
- **Radio System:** Connect FlySky receiver and configure channels.

See [`/docs/build_guide.md`](docs/build_guide.md) and [`/docs/mission_planner_setup.md`](docs/mission_planner_setup.md) for detailed steps.

## Challenges & Future Improvements

- **Challenges:** Weight balancing, waterproofing, servo integration, flight tuning over water.
- **Planned Improvements:**
  - AI-based waste detection for automation.
  - Solar charging for longer missions.
  - Enhanced waterproofing and durability.
  - Increased payload for larger cleanups.
